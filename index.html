About me

Hi! My name is Alejandro Sanchez and I am a graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University. My research interests are in high energy particle physics. I currently work for the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment at CERN, located in Geneva, Switzerland. At CERN, we use the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) to accelerate protons very close to the speed of light and collide them, creating new particles. My programming skills include Python, C++, Java, Bash, ROOT, LaTeX, and knowledge of the CMS Software (CMSSW) framework.
